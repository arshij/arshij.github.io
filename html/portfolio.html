Heat Explosion Model by the Dirac Delta Function on a π × π Plate

The Dirac delta function is special generalized function defined as infinity at one point and zero everywhere else. As a result, it's a great way to model impulse behavior (i.e., a sudden explosion of heat concentrated at a single point). I solved the 2D heat equation with fixed boundaries on a square plate to demonstrate how the temperature profile progresses into the future with heat explosions concentrated at specific points, and modeled those explosions with the Dirac delta function.
